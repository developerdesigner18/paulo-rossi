Stretched thin

Things started falling apart in February 1980. The two conspirators had over extended themselves financially; they were betting on credit with their bookies.

02

DEBTS OWED

02/06

debts owed

The “bookies” were the Mafia. The Cosa Nostra wanted to square the debts owed to them and threatened to kill the two desperate gamblers.

turn yourself in

Unbelievably, Trinca and Cruciani went
to the authorities for protection and namely to seek compensation from the players and that they be charged for fraud. Incredibly, they also sold their story Corriere dello Sport.The authorities were coming after the players.

04/06

THE INDICtMENTS ARE IN

Multiple (33) players and three  club executives were arrested. It was a shock to the country,  the “Golden Boy” Paulo Rossi, was implicated and arrested as well.

05/06

not guilty but guilty

Rossi was found “not guilty” due to a lack of evidence. However, Rossi was found “guilty” by  Italian Football Federation and was suspended for three years.

The 1982 World Cup was a distant dream.

06

A REPRIEVE FROM SERIE A

06/06

REPRIEVE TO SERIE A

In spite of the 3 year ban, Rossi was repurchased by Juventus in 1981, and he returned to the starting line-up just in time for the end of the 1981–82 season to contribute to the club’s Serie A title.

An important person from the past sticks his neck out and  transforms  Rossi’s life.
